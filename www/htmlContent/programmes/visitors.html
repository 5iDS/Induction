Welcome to Phola

As a visitor to Phola, we extend a warm welcome to you. We wish your stay with us, be both a safe and pleasant experience.

Therefore, we would like to take this time to make you aware of your responsibilities during your stay at our operation as well as what you can expect from us at Phola.

This induction programme has been created to familiarise you with the key features of our operation and the part you will play in ensuring we remain a health & safety-focused operation that cares for the environment.

On completion of the programme,
we will ask you several questions.

your life and that of your colleagues

We believe in the concept of Zero Harm to our employees, visitors, our stakeholders and the environment.

The nature of our business means that our people and we include you as Phola People, can be exposed to a number of hazards that could compromise your physical health and safety.

For this reason, the health and safety of our people
is and always will be our first priority.

We firmly believe that no one should be injured whilst working for us and that all employees, visitors and contractors should be able to return home safe and well at the end of the day.

We must bring to your attention that breaching of our Health Safety and Environment rules will result in immediate removal from site.

On completion of this induction you may move about the Administration Complex, conduct and attend meetings or presentations.
When doing so you must at all times remain within a 10 meter radius from the person escorting you and you must adhere to all legal instructions.

Should any further work or activity be necessary, you must then undergo our employee induction programme and ensure that you are in possession of all the required equipment and Personal Protective Equipment (PPE).

The following presentation will discuss hazards and risk you may experience and the rules and procedures you must follow to ensure your safety while on site at Phola.

1 - Surface Mobile Equipment or SME



Risk Rules
Large mobile machinery with restricted visibility and limited response time.
  • Keep a safe distance.
  • Get acknowledgement from the operator before assuming they have seen you.
  • Wear high visibility reflective clothing.

2 - Moving Machinery



Risk Rules
Equipment which can seriously injure or trap - examples are overheard cranes, crushers and conveyors.
  • Do not touch or approach any moving or non-isolated equipment.
  • Assume all equipment to be live and able to start at any moment.

3 - Working at Heights



Risks Rules
  • Fall from elevated platforms and structures or into excavations.
  • Fall over high walls.
  • Objects falling from height.
  • Only venture onto platforms protected by handrails.
  • Do not approach closer than 2 meters from any unprotected edge.


Take note:
This induction does not allow you to conduct any work or inspection that requires the wearing of a fall arrest harness.

4 - Water storage facilities



Risk Rules
Drowning
  • Do not approach water closer than 10 meters.


Take note:
This induction does not allow you to conduct any work or inspection that requires the wearing of a floatation device.

5 - Electricity



Risk Rules
Electric shock or electrocution from faulty or incorrectly isolated equipment.
  • Always lock-out and isolate before working on electrical equipment.
  • Always test before you start working to ensure that there are no hazards.


Take note:
This induction does not allow you to lock-out and isolate.

6 - Chemicals



Risk Rules
Acute injury or chronic occupational disease from over exposure.
  • Never work with chemicals without PPE or Material Safety Data Sheets.

Health Hazards at Phola:



Noise exceeding 85 dBa - high noise levels that can result in permanent or temporary hearing loss.
Wear hearing protection wherever you see the signs telling you to do so.

Health Hazards at Phola:



Coal dust in fine particles can cause lung disease which results in permanent disability.
Wear your dust mask wherever you see the signs telling you to do so.

We also have Plant access rules at Phola - these are as follows

You may not enter Phola under the influence of alochol or illegal substances - we conduct random alcohol and drug testing at our plant.

You may not bring any alcohol or illegal substance onto the site.

You may not bring or use any firearms / weapons on site.

All persons shall be enrolled onto the Phola Access Control System prior to entry onto the site.

Please ensure that you have the correct PPE for the area onto which you are going and that the PPE is in good condition.


  • The site induction is valid for a period of 6 Months.
  • On entering the plant, please follow the signs to your correct destination.
  • If you are going to perform a task on site please ensure that it is a task for which you have been declared competent, and you have completed the employee's induction process.
  • Only authorised vehicles and approved equipment are allowed into Phola.

At Phola we also have our
Personal Safety set of rules, which we call S L A M


  • Stop - Before you enter any area at Phola.
  • Look - Take a look at the work area, the equipment and the activities going on about you.
  • Assess - If there is anything that will present danger to you and what the source of that danger is.
  • Manage - Take steps to ensure that the area is safe to enter or that the work is made safe for you to proceed.

These actions are particularly important for any visitor in order to ensure their safety, and not to endanger the lives of anyone else.

Our Phola Life Saving Rules
mean you must never do the following:


  • Tamper with safety devices.
  • Violate lock-out or isolation procedures.
  • Enter any barricaded area.
  • Enter the workplace under the influence of alcohol or drugs.

Our Phola Life Saving Rules
mean you must never do the following:


  • Operate equipment that you are not authorised, or competent to operate.
  • Work or instruct anyone to work under unsafe conditions or against Phola standards or rules.
  • Operate any equipment unless a pre-use inspection has been completed and unsafe or potentially unsafe conditions have been rectified.

We at Phola have identified key hazard controls called our Fatal Risk Controls. Find out more these specific controls and apply where relevant.

There are other General Health, Safety & Environmental rules at Phola
and these are as follows:

  • Obey all traffic rules and road signs.
  • Speed on haul roads not to exceed 40 km/h.
  • Speed on office and plant areas not to exceed 25 km/h.
  • Headlights must be switched on at all times when driving on our site.
  • Only licensed or approved operators may ride on the Plant roads.

Safety belts must be worn at all times by everyone in the vehicle, when that vehicle is in operation.

Focus on high visibility and slower speeds in case of low visibility e.g. fog or rain.

Observe good housekeeping procedures:



  • Do not litter.
  • Dispose of waste into correct containers.

Report Spills.

In the event of a Near Miss
you must do the following:



Please report any unsafe condition, unsafe behaviour or suspected harmful environmental aspects to your Phola host, your escort or the HSE Personnel.

The following should also be reported:


  • Any injuries sustained.
  • Damage to property or the environment.
  • Any health hazards and/or unsafe physical conditions.

You have the right to refuse to work in an area, or carry out work which you believe to be unsafe.

Wearing of Personal Protective Equipment
or PPE is essential at Phola. You must wear Basic Personal Protective Equipment when accessing the site areas outside of the meeting and administrative areas - these are:


  • Hard Hat
  • Safety Spectacles
  • Overalls
  • Reflective Clothing
  • Gloves
  • Safety Boots
  • Metatarsal Guards

Other types of PPE will be required by the hazards identified in your risk assessment with your host - examples are:


  • Use lifelines when required for working at heights or in confined spaces.
  • Dust Masks, for dusty areas.
  • Hearing Protection, for high nose zones.

Our Emergency Response plans are as follows:

Emergency Evacuation of buildings and facilities:


  • Notice for evacuation will be through word of mouth or hand held radio in the field and a siren in our office areas.
  • Please follow the indicated route to the emergency gathering point as briefed by your host.
  • Withdraw in an orderly manner; walk do not run.
  • Please co-operate with the site personnel at all times during the evacuation.

Do Not Attempt to waste time by first gathering all your valuables.
Remain at the evacuation point, with your escort until the all clear has been given and it is safe to return.
